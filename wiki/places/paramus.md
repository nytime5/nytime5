## Public Profile

**Paramus** is a borough in Bergen County, New Jersey, located approximately twenty miles northwest of Midtown Manhattan. It is best known for its concentration of retail shopping — the borough contains more retail square footage per capita than nearly any municipality in the United States — and for its blue laws, which prohibit most retail activity on Sundays, a regulation that has been continuously in effect since 1957 and that residents defend with a fervor typically associated with constitutional amendments rather than mall hours.

The borough's identity is shaped by a fundamental tension: it is a quiet, affluent suburban community that also happens to be one of the most commercially dense retail corridors on the East Coast. Residents describe Paramus as "a nice place to live," a characterization that requires ignoring the approximately seventy-eight million annual shopping visits to the borough by non-residents, a figure the Paramus Chamber of Commerce has confirmed but asked not be emphasized.

Paramus takes its municipal codes seriously. The borough's Department of Code Enforcement employs a staff widely regarded as thorough, and its inspectors have been described by contractors as "relentless but fair" and by homeowners as "relentless." Residential plumbing, electrical, and structural permits are reviewed with a rigor that some neighboring municipalities have characterized as "excessive" and that Paramus officials have characterized as "the minimum standard a civilized community should expect."

The borough's public schools are consistently ranked among the top in Bergen County. Property taxes are high. Lawns are maintained. Complaints are filed promptly and in writing.

**Personality:** Paramus is a borough that believes in systems. When a system fails, Paramus does not accept it quietly. The borough council will convene. The code will be reviewed. An ordinance will be proposed. The residents of Paramus do not experience misfortune — they experience regulatory gaps.
