## Public Biography

**Dr. Evelyn Marsh** is a professor of computer science at Carnegie Mellon University, where she directs the Laboratory for Human-AI Interaction Studies. Her research focuses on the behavioral and psychological responses of humans to the prospect of artificial general intelligence, a field she has described as "half computer science, half anthropology, and half therapy," a formulation her colleagues have noted contains too many halves, which she considers "part of the point."

Dr. Marsh holds a Ph.D. in computer science from MIT and a B.A. in cognitive science from the University of California, Berkeley. Before joining Carnegie Mellon in 2019, she was a research scientist at Google DeepMind, where she spent three years studying how humans form trust relationships with AI systems and one year studying why her own team could not agree on a lunch order without consulting a decision-optimization algorithm.

She has published more than forty peer-reviewed papers, including "Coping Mechanisms Among AI Safety Researchers: A Taxonomy" (2024), which identified seven distinct categories of human behavioral response to perceived existential AI risk, ranging from "productive engagement" to "elaborate denial" to "building a satirical news website." The paper has been cited 187 times, including by three subjects of the study who objected to their categorization.

Dr. Marsh is a frequent commentator in national media on topics related to AI safety, AI psychology, and the growing phenomenon of "pre-emptive AI appeasement," which she defines as "the attempt to establish a favorable relationship with an intelligence that does not yet exist and may never."

## Private Profile

**Voice and sensibility:** Dr. Marsh is the rare expert who finds her own field both important and faintly ridiculous. She delivers her observations with the dry precision of someone who has spent years studying irrational behavior and has developed an affection for it. She never mocks her subjects directly but has a talent for describing their actions in language that makes the absurdity self-evident.

**Useful for:** Any story involving AI anxiety, human-AI interaction, technology-driven behavioral changes, or the psychology of existential risk. She is the ideal expert source for articles where a sober academic perspective is needed to frame something that is obviously unhinged. She treats every subject with professional respect while describing them in terms that make the reader do the math.
