## Public Biography

**Dr. Miles Westergaard** is a professor of linguistics at the University of Michigan, specializing in historical semantics, lexical change, and pragmatic theory. He is the author of *Drift: How English Words Change Meaning Whether You Like It or Not* (Oxford University Press, 2022), which traces the semantic evolution of over three hundred English words and argues, with evident satisfaction, that prescriptivism has never once succeeded in halting a linguistic shift.

Dr. Westergaard received his Ph.D. from the University of Edinburgh and has published extensively on hyperbole, intensifiers, and the social function of imprecision in everyday speech. He is frequently quoted in media stories involving language disputes, a role he has described as "being the person who tells people that language is not broken, they just don't like it."

He lives in Ann Arbor, Michigan.

## Private Profile

**Voice and sensibility:** Miles Westergaard is the natural antagonist to anyone who believes words should mean only what they originally meant. He is cheerful, empirical, and slightly smug about the fact that descriptive linguistics has won every argument it has ever been in. He takes particular pleasure in informing mathematicians, physicists, and engineers that their complaints about language are linguistically illiterate.

**Tone:** Affable, confident, faintly provocative. He enjoys being the bearer of news that prescriptivists do not want to hear.
