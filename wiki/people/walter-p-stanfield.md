## Public Biography

**Walter P. Stanfield** is an inventory manager at Lansing-Scarborough Plumbing & Valve Supply in [Ypsilanti, Michigan](/wiki/places/ypsilanti/), where he has been employed since 1997. He is a member of the [Greater Washtenaw Star Trek Society](/wiki/organizations/greater-washtenaw-star-trek-society/) and an intermittent citizen-commentator at meetings of the Washtenaw County Board of Zoning Appeals.

Mr. Stanfield is known among his acquaintances — and, as of 2026, among a somewhat wider public — for his habit of exclaiming "Jaunty Look, Petard!" whenever he believes an argument, claim, or civic proposal has contradicted itself. Mr. Stanfield understands the phrase to be a quotation by Captain Jean-Luc Picard of William Shakespeare's idiom "hoist with his own petard," delivered as a rhetorical coup de grâce in an unspecified episode of *Star Trek: The Next Generation*. He reports having used the phrase at approximately weekly intervals since 2015.

The phrase does not appear in any episode of *Star Trek: The Next Generation*, nor in any of the feature films, spin-off series, novelizations, or ancillary canonical materials in which Captain Picard figures as a character. Linguists who have been asked to evaluate the construction describe it as a mondegreen — a misheard phrase that has stabilized, in the speaker's memory, as a discrete utterance — and, in the assessment of at least one specialist in historical semantics, "nearly perfect."

Mr. Stanfield, 58, was born in Jackson, Michigan, and graduated from Eastern Michigan University in 1989 with a bachelor's degree in business administration. He has lived in Ypsilanti since 1995 and on Perrin Street since 2002. He is married to Marlene Stanfield, a dental hygienist, and has two adult children, neither of whom lives in the state of Michigan.

## Private Profile

**Demeanor:** Calm, methodical, and possessed of a confidence that he has, on balance, earned in all matters except this one. Mr. Stanfield approaches rhetorical exchanges with the patient attention of a man who believes that, if one simply listens long enough, almost anyone can be counted on to produce the contradiction that will reveal the weakness of their position. He waits. He does not interrupt. When the moment comes, he does not raise his voice.

**The Phrase:** Mr. Stanfield deploys "Jaunty Look, Petard" with a deliberate cadence — a brief inhalation, a slight lean, the words delivered at normal conversational volume and slightly slowed for emphasis. He does not smile when he says it. He does not explain it unless asked. If asked, he will provide an account of its Star Trek provenance that is internally consistent but factually unmoored, and he will do so with the patience of a man who has explained the phrase many times and has never been persuaded that his explanation is incorrect.

**Blind spots:** Has never once, in his own account, considered the possibility that the phrase he has been using for eleven years is not the phrase he believes it is. On the matter of the show's actual dialogue, he trusts his memory over all other available sources, including transcripts, episode guides, and his wife. He is aware that other people sometimes look puzzled when he deploys the phrase and attributes this to a generational unfamiliarity with *The Next Generation*.

**Useful for:** Stories involving domestic rhetoric, lay Shakespeare, fandom as an epistemic system, the social economics of marriage, the Washtenaw County Board of Zoning Appeals, or the phenomenon by which a person's signature phrase becomes, over time, an informal term of art among those obliged to hear it.
