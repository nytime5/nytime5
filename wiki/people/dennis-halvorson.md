## Public Biography

**Dennis Halvorson** is a retired radiology technician and the longtime treasurer of the [Greater Washtenaw Star Trek Society](/wiki/organizations/greater-washtenaw-star-trek-society/), a fan club that meets every other Thursday in the basement fellowship hall of St. Jude's Episcopal Church in Ann Arbor, Michigan. He has served as the society's treasurer since 2011, the longest unbroken tenure in that role in the organization's history.

Mr. Halvorson, 62, worked for thirty-one years at the St. Joseph Mercy Ann Arbor hospital system before retiring in 2022. He has attended every biennial meeting of the regional Federation-canon symposium since 1998 and, in 2014, authored a short paper in the society's internal newsletter, *Subspace Quarterly*, titled "On the Admissibility of Beta-Canon Material in Chapter Debate," which has been cited, informally, in at least three subsequent internal disputes.

He is known within the society for his strict but fair adherence to its parliamentary procedure, for a collection of Federation Diplomatic Corps enamel lapel pins that he rotates weekly, and for being the member most likely to, in his own words, "just let a thing go for the sake of the group."

## Private Profile

**Demeanor:** Quiet, organized, diplomatic to a fault. Mr. Halvorson is the member of any group who keeps the spreadsheet, notices when the coffee is low, and absorbs, uncomplainingly, a disproportionate share of the group's maintenance work. He is aware, to some degree, of his own exploitation by the other members, and has decided it is not worth addressing.

**Obsessions:** The admissibility of non-televised canon material in internal club disputes. The proper storage of club records. The question, long unresolved, of whether the society should open its membership to fans of *Star Trek: Discovery*, on which he has declined to take a public position but has strong private views.

**Blind spots:** Tends to defer to the loudest or most tenured voice in any disagreement, even when he himself is correct. Has, over the course of his tenure, allowed several small matters — including the Stanfield matter — to accumulate into larger matters through non-intervention.

**Useful for:** Stories involving voluntary associations, amateur parliamentary procedure, fan communities, the sociology of retirement-age hobby clubs, or the quiet infrastructure work that sustains any organization.
